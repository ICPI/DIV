Clinical Cascade: All OU

cross all Operating Units, including age/sex disaggregations (total, by age, by sex, by age/sex).

Indicators included in this tool: HTS_TST, HTS_TST_POS, TX_NEW, TX_CURR, TB_STAT, TB_ART, TB_PREV, PMTCT_STAT, PMTCT_ART, PMTCT_EID, TX_PVLS

The pages of this dossier sometimes use “level selectors.” Level selectors have the same appearance as filters, but a different function. Level selectors can be used to change the “level” of disaggregation presented in the visualization. The principal level selectors are:

  • Sex Disag: permits the user to choose between presenting the data by sex (male, female, unknown sex) or Total (all sexes grouped together)
  • Age Disag: permits the user to choose between presenting the data by Coarse Age (<15, 15+), Fine Age Adults (15-19, 20-24, 25-29, 30-34, 35-39, 40-44, 45-49, 50+) or Fine Age Peds (<1, 1-4, 5-9, 10-14)
  • Overview:
    • The 90/90/90 cascade for the last four quarters
    • Age/sex disaggregation completeness
  • HTS: Modalities: Presents cross-sectional and trend data on HTS_TST and testing yield by modality
  • HTS: Index: Presents cross-sectional and trend data on index testing coverage, volume and yield. This chapter presents index testing “proxy” cascades (i.e. they does not use the HTS_INDEX cascade).
    • Index testing proxy cascade for adults:
      • Presumed index cases (HTS_TST_POS: adults age 15+ from modalities other than Index or IndexMod),
      • Adult contacts tested (HTS_TST: adults tested in the Index or IndexMod modality)
      • Yield among adult contacts tested (HTS_TST_POS/HTS_TST among adults in the Index or IndexMod modality)
    • Index testing proxy cascade for pediatrics:
      • Presumed index cases (HTS_TST_POS: adult females age 15+ )
      • Pediatric contacts tested (HTS_TST: pediatrics age <15 tested in the Index or IndedMod modality)
      • Yield among pediatric contacts (HTS_TST_POS/HTS_TST among pediatrics in the Index or IndexMod modality)
  • KP (Key Populations): Presents cross-sectional and trend data on HTS_TST_POS, targets vs. results, and testing yield by key populations
  • HTS_TST_POS: Presents cross-sectional and trend data on:
    • HTS_TST, targets vs. results
    • The age/sex distribution of HTS_TST_POS
  • Linkage Proxy: Presents cross-sectional and trend data using the Linkage proxy
  • Treatment: Presents cross-sectional and trend data on:
    • Targets vs. Results for TX_NEW and TX_CURR
    • The age/sex distribution of TX_NEW and TX_CURR
  • Retention Proxy: Presents cross-sectional and trend data using the Retention proxy and Net gain/loss
  • VL (Viral Load): Presents cross-sectional and trend data on:
    • TX_PVLS: Targets vs. Results
    • VL suppression
  • TB (Tuberculosis): Presents cross-sectional and trend data on:
    • The coverage of known HIV status among TB Cases (TB_STAT)
    • The coverage of ART among HIV+ TB cases (TB_ART)
    • Targets vs Results for TB_PREV
  • PMTCT: Presents cross-sectional and trend data on:
    • The coverage of known HIV status among pregnant women and ANC1 (PMTCT_STAT)
    • The coverage of ART among HIV+ pregnant women (PMTCT_ART)
    • The coverage and timing of EID among HIV-exposed infants (PMTCT_EID)

  • Caution should be exercised in the interpretation of trends of integer indicators (e.g HTS_TST_POS, TX_NEW, TX_CURR) by age/sex group. If reported age/sex groups change over time, this could cause increases or decreases in age/sex-specific trends. For example:
  • Retention proxy and Net gain/loss metrics are based on changes in TX_CURR over time. For this reason, caution should be exercised in interpreting these metrics for new or modified age/sex groups. The first period that an age/sex group is reported, the Retention proxy will be extremely high because the TX_CURR for that group changed from zero (it was not reported) to a positive number (it is reported).
